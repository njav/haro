Welcome To HaRO Established Since 2016

HaRO is a non governmental,not-for profit organisation working to alleviate suffering and reduce vulnerabilities.

It draws its mandate from the numerous consequences of conflict on the Cameroonian society.

HaRO was created in 2016 and legalised by the Cameroonian authorities in 2018.Its activities cover the Centre, Littoral, North West, South West regions with projected expansion to the West and East regions by 2021 and the Northern regions by 2025



SOCIO POLITICAL CRISES IN THE CAMEROON SOCIETY

The devastating effects of conflict on the Cameroonian society especially the socio-political crisis in the North West and South West Regions is one of the reasons that led to the creation of HaRO. The conflict has internally displaced over 460.000 people within the two regions and to the neighbouring Littoral and West Regions according to statistics of October 2018. An unknown number of internally displaced persons are leaving in host families in Yaounde and face serious problems of food shortages, lack of adequate healthcare due to poverty. Women and children are more at risk and their level of vulnerability is exacerbated by the precarious conditions in which they leave. Host families have been overstretched leading to many more children filling the streets of the countryâ€™s major cities.



GENDER BASED VIOLENCE ISSUES

On another front, cases of gender-based violence continue to be reported with women and children suffering significantly on a daily basis. For displaced women and children, this is the source of serious social and psychological problems and very few effective programmes, facilities and services exist to identify and carter for their needs. The lack of awareness on where to seek support has caused many women and children to suffer silently from the effects of gender-based violence.
In this backdrop, HaRO has put in place a committed team of young professionals trained in social mobilisation to identify and raise the awareness of these categories of vulnerable persons on different facilities and services that can carter for their needs. The education and awareness raising campaigns target community social groups, primary and secondary schools and populated neighbourhoods.
